Twenty One Pilots have one of the messiest and most interesting discographies in modern alternative music. It is not just a straight line of studio albums. It is a web of self-released projects, regional editions, EPs, soundtrack appearances, live albums, livestream-related releases, commentary editions, one-off covers, and cinema experiences that keep extending the catalog beyond the standard album cycle.

This guide pulls everything into one place in English and treats the discography the way fans usually encounter it: not just as "albums and singles," but as a long release history with important side branches. As of **March 25, 2026**, the band's **latest standalone release** is **"Drag Path"** on streaming, released on **February 17, 2026**. Their **latest studio album** is **_Breach_**, released on **September 12, 2025**.

One quick note before we dive in: release counts vary a little depending on whether you count region-specific promo CDs, vinyl-only items, and community-cataloged projects separately. This post follows the broad fan-reference structure reflected by the Twenty One Pilots Wiki and cross-checks it against general discography listings.

It is also worth noting that **Discogs** can expand the picture further because it tracks **releases, appearances, alternate formats, and release variants** in a way that does not always line up neatly with a standard fan wiki or streaming-era discography. In other words, if you are cataloging everything Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun touched under the Twenty One Pilots name, Discogs is useful for spotting edge-case releases and format-specific issues even when the main fan-facing release story stays the same.

## Biography And Quick Facts

AllMusic's one-line description is still one of the cleanest introductions to the band: **Twenty One Pilots are a genre-blurring, hitmaking duo from Columbus, Ohio that combines elements of rock, hip-hop, punk, and reggae.** That description works because it captures the part of the band that never really changed, even as the eras got bigger and more theatrical.

<figure>
  <img src="/assets/images/top-discography/band-allmusic-portrait.jpg" alt="Twenty One Pilots portrait used for the band overview section" style="display:block;margin:1.25rem auto;border-radius:14px;max-width:100%;width:620px;" />
  <figcaption style="text-align:center;font-size:0.95rem;">A portrait-style band image that fits the more biographical side of the catalog story.</figcaption>
</figure>

- **Formed:** 2009
- **Origin:** Columbus, Ohio
- **Current core members:** Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun
- **Earlier members listed by AllMusic:** Chris Salih and Nick Thomas
- **Styles listed by AllMusic:** Alternative/Indie Rock, Emo-Pop

The group's early history starts as a Columbus project built around Tyler Joseph, then shifts into the Tyler-and-Josh duo that became the band's long-term identity. From there the catalog grows in distinct phases: the self-released years, the _Vessel_ breakthrough, the commercial explosion of _Blurryface_, the narrative and world-building ambition of _Trench_, the deliberately polished unease of _Scaled and Icy_, and the lore-heavy return arc of _Clancy_ and _Breach_.

If you want the shortest biography possible, it is this: Twenty One Pilots began as an Ohio band in 2009, evolved into the Tyler Joseph and Josh Dun duo, broke through with _Vessel_ and _Blurryface_, then turned into one of the most distinctive catalog acts of their era by refusing to separate songs, visuals, live presentation, and narrative identity.

## Songs That Define The Band

The singles list later in this post is the detailed catalog. This section is the faster answer to a different question: which songs best explain why Twenty One Pilots became Twenty One Pilots?

- **"Holding On to You"**: one of the clearest early mission statements, balancing anxious lyricism, rap cadence, and explosive hooks.
- **"Car Radio"**: the song that made their emotional intensity feel impossible to ignore.
- **"House of Gold"**: proof that the band could pivot from internal panic to warm, direct melody without losing identity.
- **"Stressed Out"**: the crossover breakthrough and still one of the defining songs of the 2010s alt-pop era.
- **"Ride"**: the other giant _Blurryface_ single, lighter on the surface but still wired with unease.
- **"Heathens"**: the soundtrack smash that widened their mainstream reach even further.
- **"Jumpsuit"**: the moment the post-_Blurryface_ return landed with full cinematic weight.
- **"Chlorine"**: one of their most replayable examples of pop craft meeting lore-era atmosphere.
- **"Shy Away"**: the polished lead single that introduced the _Scaled and Icy_ era's bright facade.
- **"Overcompensate"**: the re-entry point for the _Clancy_ era and one of the band's strongest direct-to-era mission statements.
- **"The Line"**: an important late-period outside-project song that also shows how well their style travels beyond core albums.

There is no single perfect "best songs" list for this band, but if you want an honest cross-era starter pack, that group of songs gets you surprisingly close.

### The Full Studio Album Run

1. **_Twenty One Pilots_** - 2009  
   The self-titled debut. Raw, theatrical, restless, and foundational for everything that came after.

2. **_Regional at Best_** - 2011  
   The cult favorite transitional record. Several songs were later re-recorded for _Vessel_, but the original version still has its own identity and fan loyalty.

3. **_Vessel_** - 2013  
   The major-label breakthrough point. This is where the duo's style really locks into recognizable form.

4. **_Blurryface_** - 2015  
   The mainstream explosion. This album also turned Blurryface into one of the band's most important recurring narrative figures.

5. **_Trench_** - 2018  
   The Dema era fully arrives. Sonically ambitious, lore-heavy, and one of the band's most critically respected releases.

6. **_Scaled and Icy_** - 2021  
   The bright, polished, lore-loaded record that many fans read as Dema propaganda.

7. **_Clancy_** - 2024  
   The record that brings the Dema storyline into more direct confrontation and active resistance.

8. **_Breach_** - 2025  
   The current latest studio album. It extends the post-_Clancy_ narrative and, as of March 25, 2026, stands as the newest full studio release.

## Awards And Recognition

Twenty One Pilots are not just a commercially successful band. They are also a decorated one. The quickest accurate way to frame their awards history is as a mix of **GRAMMY recognition**, **rock-chart dominance**, and **era-defining alternative crossover success**.

### Selected Major Awards

- **GRAMMY Award:** Best Pop Duo/Group Performance for **"Stressed Out"** at the **59th Annual GRAMMY Awards** in **2017**
- **GRAMMY recognition:** the band's GRAMMY artist page lists **1 win** and **3 nominations** through the **2026 GRAMMY Awards**
- **MTV Video Music Award:** **Best Rock Video** for **"Heathens"** at the **2016 MTV Video Music Awards**
- **Billboard Music Award:** **Top Rock Artist** in **2016**
- **Billboard Music Award:** **Top Rock Artist** in **2017**
- **Billboard Music Award:** **Top Rock Album** for **_Blurryface_** in **2016**
- **Billboard Music Award:** **Top Rock Album** for **_Scaled and Icy_** in **2022**
- **Billboard Music Award:** **Top Rock Song** for **"Heathens"** in **2017**

Just as important as the trophies is the broader achievement arc around them. _Blurryface_ became a landmark crossover album, **"Stressed Out"** and **"Ride"** turned into defining hits, and the band's chart history helped make them one of the key alternative acts of the 2010s and 2020s. Even outside formal awards, they have stacked unusual catalog milestones, including gold-certification records that underline how deeply their songs traveled beyond a normal album cycle.
